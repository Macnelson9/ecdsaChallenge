üéì How Digital Signatures Work in Blockchain

Digital signatures are the foundation of blockchain security. They ensure that transactions are authentic and haven't been tampered with.

  1. Key Generation: Create a cryptographic key pair (private key + public key) using elliptic curve cryptography
  2. Message Signing: Use your private key to create a unique digital signature for your message
  3. Signature Verification: Anyone can use your public key to verify the signature came from you
  4. Security: Only the holder of the private key can create valid signatures, but anyone can verify them

In Blockchain: When you send a transaction, you sign it with your private key. The network uses your public key (derived from your wallet address) to verify that you authorized the transaction.
